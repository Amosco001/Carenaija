Now I want a patient-focused redesign of the public-facing pages on CareNaija. Keep the existing data models, API routes, and database schema unchanged — this is a UI/UX overhaul, not a backend rewrite.

PRIMARY USER: A Nigerian patient or family member trying to find, evaluate, and choose a hospital. They are often on a phone, sometimes on poor network conditions, and they need to make decisions quickly under stress (sick relative, emergency, pregnancy, chronic condition).

DESIGN PRINCIPLES:
- Mobile-first. Every layout starts at 360px width and scales up.
- Trust above all. Patients are skeptical. Show real review counts, verified badges, last-updated timestamps, and source attribution.
- Speed of decision. A patient should be able to land on the homepage and within 3 taps be looking at a ranked list of hospitals near them.
- Nigerian context. Use Naira (₦) for all prices. Mention HMOs by name (Hygeia, AXA Mansard, Reliance HMO, Avon, etc). Reference Nigerian states and major cities by name.
- Calm, clinical, professional. Avoid loud gradients, generic stock photos, and "AI startup" aesthetics. Reference: NHS.uk, Healthgrades, Zocdoc — clean, readable, trustworthy.
